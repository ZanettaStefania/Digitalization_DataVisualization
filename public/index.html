The Dawn of the Digital Era

In this first section we would like to give you an historical of the emergence of computers and the internet as integral parts of our daily lives.

Individuals that never used Internet in 2012

In the early days of the internet's introduction to households, the majority of people
were not connected digitally.
Our visualization captures this shift over time, allowing users to explore the percentage of individuals who never used the internet.

This chart helps us see how internet became an integral part to our everyday life.

By adjusting the year, a clear pattern emerges, allowing us to see that the percentage of people that never used internet decrease dramaticaly. Going from an average of 23% of people in Europe that never used internet in 2012, to the more recent years where we can see that the same average oscillates from 8% to 6%.

Reasons for not having internet access at home in 2010 in Europe

As we observe the rise in household computer adoption, our third visualization delves into the evolving landscape of internet accessibility.
The treemap illustrates the primary reasons people did not have access to the internet, offering a comprehensive understanding of the barriers faced or concerns around internet access at home during the past decade.

Notably, the data presented is an average of European statistics, providing a region-wide perspective.
The treemap format allows for a visually intuitive representation, with each segment depicting a specific reason, to delve into the real percentages, you can simply hover on various sections of the chart.

Even with a notable decrease in the overall percentage of individuals without never used internet, upon adjusting the year, surprisingly, it becomes apparent that "Not needed" consistently stands out as one of the main reasons cited by people for not having internet access at home.

Moreover, over the years, certain barriers, such as "Physical disability," gradually fade away, indicating a significant improvement in accessibility.

Probability distribution of individuals using the mobile phone to access Internet in Europe

Conclusions

In summary, we embark on a journey from the early days of limited internet access to the present, where digitalization has become normalized across various aspects of our lives.

Mobile devices, particularly mobile phones, have played a pivotal role in this transformation, witnessing a significant surge in usage for internet access.

Technology is quickly evolving, and it appears that people are learning and adapting just as swiftly.
